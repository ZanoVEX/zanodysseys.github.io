East Zanzibar

Wide tidal beaches, turquoise lagoons, and village life shaped by the ocean’s rhythm.

Paje, Jambiani & Michamvi

The east coast of Zanzibar is defined by tides, trade winds, and open horizons. Life here moves with the ocean — from early morning fishing to sunset walks across endless white sand.

Paje

Energy, water sports, and youthful island spirit

Paje Beach

South-East Zanzibar

Paje is known for its vibrant energy and strong connection to the sea. A hub for kitesurfing and social beach life, it blends laid-back vibes with adventurous spirit.

  • Kitesurfing and wind sports
  • Wide tidal beach walks
  • Beach cafés and social life
  • Creative and international community
  • Great base for excursions

Jambiani

Village life, calm beaches, and authentic Swahili rhythm

Jambiani Village

East Zanzibar Coast

Jambiani is a peaceful fishing village where daily life unfolds along the shoreline. Ideal for travelers seeking cultural connection, simplicity, and long beach walks.

  • Traditional fishing culture
  • Quiet, uncrowded beaches
  • Community-based tourism
  • Seaweed farming traditions
  • Relaxed pace of life

Michamvi

Secluded beauty and sunset-facing lagoons

Michamvi Peninsula

East Zanzibar Peninsula

Michamvi offers a quieter, more secluded side of the east coast. With calm lagoons and sunset views, it is perfect for romance, reflection, and slow travel.

  • Calm lagoons and shallow waters
  • Sunset beaches on the east coast
  • Romantic and peaceful atmosphere
  • Ideal for couples and retreats
  • Close to nature and marine life
